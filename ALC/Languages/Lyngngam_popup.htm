Nombre locuteurs / Number of speakers

About 72 000 native speakers (in the so-called West Khasi Hills and about 2500 in Bangladesh)
Classification / Classification

austro-asiatique > khmuic-palaungic > pnaric-war-lyngngam > pnar; khasi; war; lyngngam
lyngngam > rongrin + dgir + nongtraa
Parlers composites à dominante lyngngam : langrin influencé par le pnar ; langkma influencé par le khasi standard ; différentes variétés influencées par le garo (TB) comme le shallang. D'autres variétés sont parlées par un groupe d'environ 25 000 locuteurs dans le Bengladesh.

Austro-Asiatic > Khmuic-Palaungic >Pnaric-War-Lyngngam > Pnar; Khasi; War; Lyngngam
Lyngngam > Rongrin + Dgir + Nongtraa
Composite varieties: Langrin influenced by Pnar: Langrin, Marksuin ; Langkma influenced by Standard Khasi: Langkma; many different varieties of Lyngngam influenced by Garo (TB) are spoken in Nongstoin, in Shillong and near each side of the boarder of the Khasi and Garo constituencies like Shalang in the West Khasi Hills. Other varieties are spoken in Bangladesh by around 25 000 speakers.
