  • Mr Ergin ÖPENGIN has defended his thesis on Clitic/affix interactions: a corpus-based study of person marking in the Mukri variety of Central Kurdish, "cotutelle" PhD between Paris III (F. Jacquesson) & University of Bamberg, Germany (G. Haig)
    on Monday 16 September 2013 at 9:00
    at the Maison de la Recherche (room Claude Simon)
    4 rue des Irlandais 75005 Paris (see also at the Paris III website)
    Jury:
    Mrs Agnès Korn, PRU, Universiy of Frankfurt - Germany
    Mr Claude Hagège, Professeur Honoraire at Collège de France
    Mr Geoffrey Haig, PRU, University of Bamberg - Germany (Supervisor)
    Mr François Jacquesson, DR CNRS - University of Sorbonne Nouvelle Paris 3 (Supervisor)
    Mrs Annie Montaut, PRU - INALCO
    Mrs Pollet Samvelian, PRU - University Sorbonne Nouvelle Paris 3
    Mr Franck Seifart, Senior Researcher Max Planck Institute - Leipzig - Germany
    Abstract:
    The person-marking system of Central Kurdish, with a complex set of person-marker paradigms, presents a number of problems, especially in the manners in which different person-marker paradigms are distributed for argument-indexing. For instance, a pronominal complement of an adposition is a person form from the paradigm of clitic person markers in a present-tense construction, but the formal expression of the same argument is switched to verbal affix person marker in a past-tense construction. Previous scholarship (Bynon 1979; Samvelian 2007; Haig 2008; Jügel 2009) has pointed to the relevant phenomena and provided important descriptive facts, but, a comprehensive treatment of the problem and an inclusive explanation to possible structural motivations behind it are still lacking. Exploration of this specific problem of the morphosyntax of Central Kurdish requires a thorough and comprehensive analysis of its person-marking system. Thus, morphophonological status of respective person marking paradigms is established and argument-indexing function personmarker paradigms are thoroughly investigated, showing striking deviations from dominant cross-linguistic tendencies in associating 'grammatical agreement' with affixes and 'pronominal agreement' or 'cross-reference' with 'clitics' (Siewierska 2004). A novel analysis of clitic placement in Central Kurdish is proposed whereby a clitic is considered to be occurring systematically following a prosodic word. The occurrence of clitic person markers on hosts of diverse syntactic and morphological status, such as syntactic phrases, morphological words and verbal inflectional morphemes (i.e. 'endocliticisis' in Samvelian 2007) is reduced to differences in the type of cliticization (Selkirk 1995). The formal switch from a clitic to a verbal affix person marker in the expression of a number of arguments is analysed as occurring for avoiding certain clitic combinations (Gerlach 2002) since the latter inevitably leads to an improperly placed clitic. A number of other problems relating to seeminlgy non-clitic-like and/or idiosyncratic manifestations of clitic person markers are explained through a closer investigation of the prosodic structure of the forms in question and within larger constraints (Prince and Smolensky 1993; Yip 1998) of expressing the intended arguments by preserving distinct morphological and phonological identity of the person forms in question.
