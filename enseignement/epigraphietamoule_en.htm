Eighth International Workshop on Tamil Epigraphy,
Tamil Nadu, India, 11-22 July 2011


for more informations, clic on the poster (or in pdf)

 

We are pleased to annonce our Eighth International Workshop on Tamil Epigraphy. This workshop is intended for students and researchers who have little or no previous experience with the discipline. The workshop will focus on how to read and interpret inscriptions and the tools to achieve them. The lectures and discussions are planned to highlight two facts: 1) Inscriptions constitute the historical source material and 2) a mustidisciplinary approach is necessary for the extraction of information.

Please note that the workshop will be held in Pondicherry and in Tanjavur in Tamil Nadu, India. The workshop will lay more attention on filed works and practical trainings: estampage making, preservation, reading in situ, and palaeography. Invited scholars and epigraphists will share their work in a series of afternoon lectures on topics related to Tamil and South Indian studies..
Course coordinators:
Appasamy Murugaiyan (École Pratique des Hautes Études - UMR 7528 Mondes Iranien et Indien) and Prof. K. Rajan (Pondicherry University)

2011 course programme
– History of Tamil epigraphy, the Tamil epigraphic culture.
– Evolution of Tamil scripts.
– Estampage making, digital documentation and preservation of inscriptions.
– Structure of epigraphic texts, methods of analysis and interpretation.
– Religious, historical and social issues raised in various types of inscriptions.
– Linguistic features of different types of inscriptions.
– Philological study of personal names.

Registration
No registration fees are required, but each participant has to bear the cost of practical works and travelling to different sites. Interested scholars need to register before 15th June 2011.
For registration and information, please contact:
A. Murugaiyan (e-mail).

• « Sixth International Workshop on Tamil Epigraphy »
29 June-10 July 2009
Paris, Ecole Pratique des Hautes Etudes, Sciences historiques et philologiques Department

Course coordinator: Appasamy MURUGAIYAN (EPHE-SHP)
2009 course programme
1. Initiation (histoire de l'épigraphie tamoule, culture épigraphique tamoule ; évolution des écritures tamoules ; technique d'estampage, archivage numérique et préservation des inscriptions ; structure des textes épigraphiques, méthodes d'analyse et d'interprétation)
2. Travaux consacrés à l'étude de textes épigraphiques tamouls des dynasties de Chola, de Pallava, de Pandya et de Vijayanagara (VIIe au XVIe siècles). Pour chaque type de texte, nous proposons une analyse pluridisciplinaire portant sur les aspects religieux, historiques et sociaux en insistant sur les particularités linguistiques (morphologie et syntaxe), en comparaison avec le tamoul moderne. Une analyse philologique des noms propres est aussi proposée.
