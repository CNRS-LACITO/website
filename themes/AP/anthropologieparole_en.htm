Anthropology of Speaking

 

The study of communication phenomena is situated at a crossing between several distinct perspectives. The questions raised belong not only to the domain of social anthropology and the study of language and its uses, but also to the domain of linguistics and the study of verbal interactions, cognition (situational and incarnate), as well as to that of ethnomusicology and the study of how music is anchored in verbal communication, and not lastly to the domain of semiotics and the study of multimodal forms of communication. In each of these cases, the reflections are necessarily interdisciplinary.

The methodologies applied to the study fields belong both to ethnography (participative observation) and to those that apply to the specific objects studied (audio and video recordings, etc.). Field work is carried out in Africa (Cameroon, Chad, Kenya, Comoros), in Central America (Guatemala), in South America (Colombia), in the Caribbean, in Europe (Countries of France, Romania, Italy). The studies are not limited to rural areas, but equally concern urban and industrial communities (far or near). Thus the social, linguistic and musical factors studied within this theme are extremely varied.
