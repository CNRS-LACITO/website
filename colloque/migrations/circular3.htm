Colloque "Migrations" - Workshop Migrations

5-7 septembre 2007, Porquerolles (Var, France)
coord. F. Jacquesson

– 3rd Circular letter – 30 July 2007

Dear colleagues,

 

1/ The practical point of view : trip
We hope you already reserved your trip tickets. We will be waiting for you in Toulon railway station, September 4th. Please be there at 17h00 because we need one full hour to reach the harbour and the last boat starts at 18h30 ; note that trains from Paris arriving at 17h29 and 17h35 will be too late (see www.sncf.fr ). The station is within walking distance of Toulon center and the sea, but there is no cloakroom in Toulon railway station.
You can also reach Porquerolles by yourself: from Toulon, a first bus to Hyères, then another to La Tour Fondue; from La Tour Fondue, the boat to Porquerolles. From Porquerolles harbour, IGESA center is within walking distance, not far from the church.
If you arrive late, please let us know.
A small bus will be waiting for us from Toulon to La Tour Fondue on Sept 4 (see above), and on Sept 7 from La Tour Fondue to Toulon railway station in the beginning of the afternoon.
Keep plane and train tickets with you: we will need them to reimburse your trips.

2/ The practical point of view : accomodation
All participants will be accomodated in IGESA center, where the workshop will take place. The list gives the number of persons to be accomodated and indicates if you requested a single or double room. If this is wrong, tell us as soon as possible.
Particpants will be accomodated from Sept 4's night to Sept 6's night. These nights will be paid by the Workshop. We will leave IGESA center Sept 7 afternoon.
Our first meal together will be Sept. 4's dinner, and then on until Sept 7's lunch. These meals will be paid of by the Workshop.

3/ The scientific (but still practical) side
A videoprojector will be with us. If you use Powerpoint presentations, please have your presentation ready on a USB pendrive. If your presentation uses special fonts, make sure these will accompany your presentation. If you use paper presentations (handouts), please bring all necessary copies with you: there is no easy photocopying in Porquerolles. If you have other/specific requests, let us know in advance.
Remember there will be no paper edition (unless you loudly protest) of your contributions. We intend to put your contributions on the Workshop Webpage, as soon as possible, ideally in the days that immediately follow the workshop. This means you have to think of it. (1) Either your contribution is quite ready for this e-publishing, understandable without further editing, (2) or your prefer to put on the Web page a different contribution, with additional explanations, or after "secret information" has been deleted. In this second case, please try to prepare your web-page presentation in advance, so that it can be put on the web immediately after the workshop.

4/ The scientific side
This workshop is funded by the European Science Foundation, to which we are thankful. Eva Hoogland, from the ESF staff, will be with us and we hope she will describe what is ESF, and the program the workshop is included in.
Each talk (20 to 30 mn) will be immediately followed by discussion, except if time problems appear. The earlier plan in 6 topics has been somewhat altered, but still something of it clearly emerges. Anyway the basic formula remains the same : we will try to think about "What is a migration?", be critical on some simplified views, be constructive in bringing examples that build a (perhaps) less passionate or (certainly) more informed view. Once again, remember this is a multi-disciplinary workshop, not a specialist conference, and the aim is to think about migrations, the way it is perceived and lived, or instrumentalized as a fact or as a concept.

5/ The not-scientific side
Porquerolles is a nice (and small) place. We will work in the morning and after lunch will have some time free (the Mediterranean siesta) before talks begin again. It is quite possible to walk and cycle in many places. But not all places because Porquerolles is also something like a natural reserve. We understand there some fort somewhere used for scientific bat breeding which is normally not acessible - this sort of thing. Transportation by car are rather restricted in the island. Normally, beginning of September is a fine season, and it is quite possible to swim in the sea. Except if it is becoming windy, then it is better to have something warmer than a bathsuit.


François Jacquesson, CNRS
