What to do now ?

Could you give a title for your communication before July the 15th ?

We remind you the last lines of our October 2006 description :

  • This workshop would like to invite scholars to describe enlightening cases of migrations, wherever in the world and in the period they find most interesting, in order to improve our knowledge of what we mean by "migration". And to help assembling fine-grained examples which would remind fellow researchers, or a wider audience, that migrations do exist, but are to be analysed with historical caution.

  • Historians and prehistorians, anthropologists and linguists, demographers and geographers, political scientists and population geneticists are all concerned by these thematic reflections - at once methodological and comparative - to which we invite.

You are perfectly free to choose any perspective and examples you like best, provided they can usefully contribute to a better understanding of what "Migrations" can be, and a better methodology to understand the phenomenon in its complexity.

We will follow our program, which is more or less divided in 6 sections.
The schedule had to be adapted to actual proposals by our colleagues. See the other attached document.

Please remember that your colleagues from other disciplines may be grateful, if you are kind enough to put your explanations and descriptions in a context that make them easily understandable.

Publication
We think that the best (and quickest) way to make the workshop useful for a wider audience is a website, on which your contributions (shortened text, text, edited text, text + images or Powerpoint – up to you) would be accessible.
Since the idea is to make your contributions accessible as soon as possible after the workshop, please think of it in advance.

Transportation
The island of Porquerolles is south of Hyères and not far from Toulon.
You may reach the island by yourself (preferably Sept. the 4th, evening) or join the coach which will wait for you near Toulon railway station (Sept. 4th, the exact time will be fixed later) ; the coach will take us to La Tour Fondue, the place where we take the boat to Porquerolles.
You may arrive:

  • by plane to the Toulon/Hyères airport, see http://www.airfrance.fr ; the only way to reach La Tour Fondue from the airport is taxi.

  • by train to Toulon railway station, see www.sncf.com ; you can also reach Hyères railway station, if you want to arrive closer to La Tour Fondue.

If you want to arrange the complete trip by yourself, boat included, you will have to reach either:

  • Port (Harbour) of La Tour Fondue (crossing 15 mn)
    Compagnie TLV / TVM, 04 94 58 21 81.

  • Port du Lavandou (crossing 50 mn)
    Compagnie des Iles d'Or: 04 94 71 01 02.

  • Port de Toulon (crossing 1 h)
    Contact Office de Tourisme de Toulon : 04 94 18 53 00.

Trips will be refunded by ESF: please keep all tickets with you.

Accomodation and meals will be taken care of by the organizer, from Sept. 4th dinner to Sept. 7th lunch. They will take place in the IGESA center, within walking distance to Porquerolles harbour.

If you intend to come with husband/wife or children, or to extend you stay in the island, please : (1) contact IGESA to make sure it is still possible (there are also a few hotels in Porquerolles, but they are usually booked rather early). Contact adress is in the IGESA website mentionned above. (2) When you are sure it is possible, please let me know.

Thank you very much.


François Jacquesson, CNRS
