Colloque "Steppes" - Workshop Steppes

29-30 septembre et 1er octobre 2007, Porquerolles (Var, France)
coord. F. Jacquesson

– 2nd Circular letter – May 9th 2007

Dear colleagues,

It is now time to think of your talk in Porquerolles, and maybe also of your trip to and from.

 

The venue
The Porquerolles venue is now decided.
If you do not know exactly where Porquerolles is, you can have a look at the map included in the IGESA website, and click on the southernmost location.
For transportation, see the bottom of the page.

Our participant list is not quite completed. We presently have 17 participants from China, France, Germany, Great-Britain, Russia and Sweden. They are anthropologists, archaeologists, historians, linguists.
You will soon find fuller details on the Lacito Website ;
The 1st circular is presently available at :
http://lacito.vjf.cnrs.fr/colloque/steppes/circular1.htm

 

What to do now ?

Could you give a title for your talk before July the 10th ?
If you can think of an abstract as well, excellent. Otherwise I shall remind you about the abstract after some time.

Here are the last lines of our November 2006 description :

  • This workshop would like, not only to reaffirm these simple views against recurrent simplifications, but also to compare the experience of historians, archaeologists, anthropologists, linguists and geneticists who have been working in this huge area. We would like participants to choose in their experience illuminating cases in order to improve the ideas we may entertain, either because we work in another discipline, or because we work in a different place. For instance : How far is the Chinese Long Wall comparable to the Roman Limes ? How similar are the invaders of Gengis Khan to those associated with Tamerlan or Attila? Was the social or economical background the same? Is climatic history a sufficient explanation for the East-to-West movement of herders? How far can we figure out common cultural features for all (or parts) of these peoples? Are the common points only a fantasy born elsewhere and for aims quite alien to the ‘steppes’? These are only examples among the many questions that arose from a panoramic study of the identities in the “world of the steppes”.

You are perfectly free to choose any perspective and examples you like best, provided they can usefully contribute to a better understanding of what "Steppes" can be, and a better methodology to understand them.

Please remember that your colleagues from other disciplines may be grateful, if you are kind enough to put your explanations and descriptions in a context that make them easily understandable.

 

Publication
We think that the best (and quickest) way to make the workshop useful for a wider audience is a website, on which your contributions (shortened text, text, edited text, text + images or Powerpoint – up to you) would be accessible.
Since the idea is to make your contributions accessible as soon as possible after the workshop, please think of it in advance.

Transportation
The island of Porquerolles is south of Hyères and not far from Toulon.
You may reach the island by yourself (preferably Sept. the 28th, evening) or join the coach which will wait for you near Toulon railway station (Sept. 28th, the exact time will be fixed later) ; the coach will take us to La Tour Fondue, the place where we take the boat to Porquerolles.
You may arrive:

  • by plane to the Toulon/Hyères airport, see http://www.airfrance.fr ; the only way to reach La Tour Fondue from the airport is taxi.

  • by train to Toulon railway station, see www.sncf.com ; you can also reach Hyères railway station, if you want to arrive closer to La Tour Fondue.

If you want to arrange the complete trip by yourself, boat included, you will have to reach either:

  • Port (Harbour) of La Tour Fondue (crossing 15 mn)
    Compagnie TLV / TVM, 04 94 58 21 81.

  • Port du Lavandou (crossing 50 mn)
    Compagnie des Iles d'Or: 04 94 71 01 02.

  • Port de Toulon (crossing 1 h)
    Contact Office de Tourisme de Toulon : 04 94 18 53 00.

Plane and trips will be refunded by ESF: please keep all tickets with you.

The participants who will use the coach from Toulon to La Tour Fondue and then the crossing to Porquerolles will have to pay for the fare.

Accomodation and meals will be taken care of by the organizer, from Sept. 28th dinner to Oct. 1st lunch. They will take place in the IGESA center, within walking distance to Porquerolles harbour.

If you intend to come with husband/wife or children, or to extend you stay in the island, please : (1) contact IGESA to make sure it is still possible (there are also a few hotels in Porquerolles, but they are usually booked rather early). Contact adress is in the IGESA website mentionned above. (2) When you are sure it is possible, please let me know.

Thank you very much.


François Jacquesson, CNRS
