Nepali: a brief note

 

Nepali is an Indo-Aryan language of the Eastern Pahari group spoken in Nepal, where it is the official language, and in hilly regions to the east of Nepal, in the Indian states of West Bengal (Darjiling district) and Sikkim.

The language originated in the western part of present-day Nepal, and that is where the greatest dialectal diversity is found today. The samples presented on this site are in the dialect of Accham, Seti Zone, in far-western Nepal, with some admixture of standard Nepali. The Accham dialect is not usually written, and it has not been the object of scientific study to our knowledge.

The stories presented here were recorded by Marie Leconte-Tilouine, an anthropologist member of the CNRS research group "Himalaya", who had them transcribed locally in Devanagari. They were translated into French in Paris, using her field notes.

The Old Woodcutter
Nepal, Surkhet, Ghatgaun, 2002, Dholi Laphe, Marie Lecomte-Tilouine

Tiger and Jackal / [nep] Bag ra syal
Nepal, Seti Zone, Achham District, Dhanku VDC, Dungala, 2007, "DB" (anonyme), Marie Lecomte-Tilouine

Traditional story recorded to illustrate a dialect of Nepali spoken in Achham district, Nepal. The speaker is an elderly Brahman woman. The story is told in some haste.

The Cretin Brother (1) / [nep] Lato Bhai
Nepal, Seti Zone, Achham District, Dhanku VDC, Dungala, 2007, conteur anonyme, Marie Lecomte-Tilouine

Traditional story recorded to illustrate a dialect of Nepali spoken in Achham district, Nepal. The speaker is an elderly Brahman woman. The story may be also be known under the name "The long-haired girl" (Nep. "Lamcultheni").

The Cretin Brother (2) / [nep] Lato Bhai
Nepal, Seti Zone, Achham District, Bayala VDC, Bajuda, 2007, Lal Shobha Tamata, Marie Lecomte-Tilouine

Humorous story recorded to illustrate a dialect of Nepali spoken in Achham. The narrator is a woman over 50 years old of an artisan caste.

The Hare and the Jackal / [nep] Saso ra syal
Nepal, Seti Zone, Achham District, Bayala VDC, Bajuda, 2007, Lal Shobha Tamata, Marie Lecomte-Tilouine

Humorous story recorded to illustrate a dialect of Nepali spoken in Achham. The narrator is a woman over 50 years old of an artisan caste.

 

Boyd Michailovsky
